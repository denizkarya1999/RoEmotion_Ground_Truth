HEALTHY RESTING HEART-RATE REFERENCE

American Heart Association. "All About Heart Rate (Pulse)."
https://www.heart.org/en/health-topics/high-blood-pressure/the-facts-about-high-blood-pressure/all-about-heart-rate-pulse
Accessed July 12, 2026.

Used in bpm_gui.py for the adult resting-heart-rate reference bands:
  Low: below 60 BPM
  Typical: 60-100 BPM
  High: above 100 BPM

The source states that a normal resting heart rate is 60-100 BPM when a
person is sitting or lying down, calm, and feeling well. These ranges are
general adult resting references and are not diagnostic. Heart rate varies
with activity, fitness, medication, health, stress, and other factors; for
example, some trained athletes have resting rates below 60 BPM.
